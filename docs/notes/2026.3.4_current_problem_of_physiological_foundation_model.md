# Current Problem of Physiological Foundation Model

## Development Path of EEG foundation model

1. Differentiation of Model Scope
    - General-purpose models: In the early stages of EEG foundation model development, the focus was on creating general-purpose models that could handle a wide range of tasks and applications. These models were designed to create a "Universal" presentation of EEG data.
    - Specialized models: As the field progressed, researchers began to develop paradigm-specific models that perform well on specific tasks, such as MIRepNet for motor imagery tasks and EpilepsyFM for epilepsy detection.
2. The enrichment of Signal Modality
    - Though EEG is the primary signal modality for these foundation models, there has been an increasing interest in incorporating additional modalities, such as fMRI, MEG, and ECoG, to enhance the performance and robustness of the models. Multimodal Joint Pre-training is a promising direction for future research in this area.
3. The development of Model Architecture
    - About 82% of the EEG foundation models are based on the Transformer architecture, which has shown great success in various domains.
    - In order to deal with the efficiency issue of long EEG sequences, State Space Models like Mamba have been applied to EEG foundation models.
4. Deepening of Pre-training Strategies
    - Over 70% of the EEG foundation models utilize Masked Reconstruction as their pre-training strategy.
    - Development of reconstruction target: Originally, the reconstruction target was the raw EEG signal. However, raw EEG signals are noisy and contains artifacts, which can hinder the learning process. Token reconstruction and frequency reconstruction have been proposed as alternative reconstruction targets.

    > additional mark here: Token reconstruction has been adopted as the method to overcome the noise issue of raw signals. But the tokenization process can also be influenced by noise. Further investigation should be conducted to explore the impact of noise on tokenization and how to mitigate it.

## Current Challenges

1. The universal representation of EEG data is still unlearned
    - Test on current EEG foundation models shows, when pretrained encoder is frozen, the performance of downstream tasks is significantly worse than the fine-tuning setting. This indicates that the pretrained encoder can be directly applied to various downstream tasks.
2. The performance of EEG foundation models is still unsatisfactory
    - In most tasks, the performance of EEG foundation models is still worse than the SOTA task-specific models, for example, EEGNet and ShallowConv.
3. Scaling Law won't work for EEG foudation models
    - For LLMs, scaling up the model size and training data can lead to significant improvements in performance. However, for EEG foundation models, Uni-NTFM with billion-level parameters, its generalization performance is still worse than CBraMod with only 4M parameters.
4. Few-shot calibration is still a problem for EEG foundation models
    - Few-shot calibration has always been a splendid vision for EEG foundation models, however, current EEG foundation models struggle to achieve good performance in few-shot settings. When data from the target subject is limited, the performance of EEG foundation models drops significantly.
5. Data noise causes pre-training bias
    - EEG data is inherently noisy and contains various artifacts, which can lead to biased learning during the pre-training phase. The model may use a large proportion of its capacity to memorize the noise and artifacts, which can hinder its ability to learn meaningful representations of the underlying neural activity.

## Future Directions

1. Build large-scale high-quality datasets
    - The failure of scaling law in EEG foundation models may be attributed to the limited size and quality of the training data.
2. Design more Efficient Pre-training and Fine-tuning Strategies
    - Addressing cross-device heterogeneity: Develop more effective channel mapping and alignment techniques to handle electrode layout variations across different devices.
    - Enhanced pre-training target: Explore alternative pre-training targets that are less susceptible to noise and artifacts, mean while more correlated with the underlying neural activity, such as token reconstruction and frequency reconstruction.
    - Introduce distribution alignment techniques: narrowing the gap between pre-training and fine-tuning data distributions can help improve the generalization performance of EEG foundation models.

## Further Reflection

1. Physiological signal may need a different paradigm from NLP and CV
    - The development of EEG foundation models has been heavily influenced by the success of LLMs in NLP and CV. The masked token reconstruction strategy is from BERT and Image block reconstruction is from MAE. However, the unique characteristics of physiological signals may require a different paradigm for foundation model development.
      - Language possesser strict grammar and discrete semantics
      - images have strong spatial correlation and local features
      - EEG signals represents the macroscopic sysnthesis of billions of neuronal activities, spatially blurred by the skull and scalp.
2. Avoid mixture of different paradigms
    - Most EEG language models are trained on a mixture of different paradigms, such as motor imagery, sleep, epilepsy, emotion, etc. The model learns what is common across these paradigms, but how much components of these signals are shared is still unknown.
