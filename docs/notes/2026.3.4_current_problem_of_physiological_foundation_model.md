# Current Problem of Physiological Foundation Model

## Development Path of EEG foundation model

1. Differentiation of Model Scope
    - General-purpose models: In the early stages of EEG foundation model development, the focus was on creating general-purpose models that could handle a wide range of tasks and applications. These models were designed to create a "Universal" presentation of EEG data.
    - Specialized models: As the field progressed, researchers began to develop paradigm-specific models that perform well on specific tasks, such as MIRepNet for motor imagery tasks and EpilepsyFM for epilepsy detection.
2. The enrichment of Signal Modality
    - Though EEG is the primary signal modality for these foundation models, there has been an increasing interest in incorporating additional modalities, such as fMRI, MEG, and ECoG, to enhance the performance and robustness of the models. Multimodal Joint Pre-training is a promising direction for future research in this area.
3. The development of Model Architecture
    - About 82% of the EEG foundation models are based on the Transformer architecture, which has shown great success in various domains.
    - In order to deal with the efficiency issue of long EEG sequences, State Space Models like Mamba have been applied to EEG foundation models.
4. Deepening of Pre-training Strategies
    - Over 70% of the EEG foundation models utilize Masked Reconstruction as their pre-training strategy.
    - Development of reconstruction target: Originally, the reconstruction target was the raw EEG signal. However, raw EEG signals are noisy and contains artifacts, which can hinder the learning process. Token reconstruction and frequency reconstruction have been proposed as alternative reconstruction targets.

    > additional mark here: Token reconstruction has been adopted as the method to overcome the noise issue of raw signals. But the tokenization process can also be influenced by noise. Further investigation should be conducted to explore the impact of noise on tokenization and how to mitigate it.
